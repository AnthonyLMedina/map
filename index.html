Shooting Incidents in East New York for Summer Of 2020 Show Neighborhoods In Need of Gun Control

A breakdown of the NYPD 75th Precicnt responses to shooting incidents reflects a year riddled with crime.

By Anthony Medina

The NYPD continues to battle an increase in shooting incidents across New York City for a year shrouded by the coronavirus pandemic. The drastic increase in gun violence began during the summer months, from July to August, when a consistent rise in shooting incidents were recorded.

Residents in the confines of the NYPD 75th Precinct, which covers East New York and Cypress Hills, Brooklyn, are no strangers to the increased gun violence that spread across the city. Over the summer, the NYPD experienced a +130% increase in citywide shooting incidents in June, +177% increase in July, and a +166% increase in August.

Using NYPD shooting incident data, provided by the NYPD on the 311 Open Data website, the map demonstrates where shooting incidents were recorded in East New York and Cypress Hills. The data provided doesn’t include the total number of shooting incidents for the year, but provides what the NYPD revealed from the beginning of this year to November.
